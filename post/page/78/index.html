How to use I, me or myself in English

 Posted on August 17, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  945 words  |  Tandra Barner

English learners and native English speakers alike have trouble knowing when to use “I”, “me” or “myself”. The rules themselves are simple: “I” is a subject pronoun, “me” is an object pronoun and “myself” is a reflexive pronoun. Use the subject pronoun “I” when the speaker is performing the action. Use the object pronoun “me” when the speaker is receiving the action. Use “myself” when the subject and object are the same. [Read More]
