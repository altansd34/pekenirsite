RuPaul’s Drag Race star Shangela recently announced the final dates for her looming tour, Fully Lit. This brand-new show will be a 90-minute solo performance from the drag entertainer, promising to deliver fiery and fabulous acts for its audiences.

Shangela Announces Dates for Fully Lit Tour 2023

The Dancing With The Stars alum newly posted on Instagram to inform fans about the upcoming dates of her tour. Fully Lit Tour will kick off on January 19 in Boston, Massachusetts and will end on February 10 in Austin, Texas.

Throughout the event, Shangela will make stops in Washington, D.C., New York, Los Angeles and San Francisco. Fully Lit will also take place in Orlando, Florida; Raleigh, North Carolina; Glenside and Munhall, Pennsylvania; and Dallas, Texas.

“If you thought [I] was entertaining in Dancing With The Stars, if you thought you got the heart and emotion on We’re Here, if you thought the she was living for Drag Race, then baby, come on see Fully Lit,” she said on TikTok. “I’m putting it all and giving it all to you.”

According to Shangela, her tour will involve high-energy performances alongside her backup dancers. A special guest will also appear, although she didn’t mention about it further. Moreover, Fully Lit will expose never-before-told stories about DWTS, We’re Here, Drag Race, and more.

Fans Are Excited to See Her Perform Live

In her Instagram post, Shangela mentioned that the tickets have been selling quickly. Fans evidently can’t wait to witness her in Fully Lit, with fans telling her in the comment section that they have already bought tickets for the tour. Meanwhile, others are disappointed that she will not make a stop in cities near them.

“Come to CANADA, too,” one user wrote. “You are loved up here in the North.”

Before the show, she will have a meet and greet session with her supporters. This will give Shangela the opportunity to interact with her fans more. Additionally, a tour merch will also be available for fans to purchase.

“If you’ve ever been to a Shangela show, u know it’s gonna be a good time! See y’all soooon,” she wrote.
