Macaulay Culkin and Brenda Song Secretly Welcomed Their Second Child Months Ago

 Posted on August 7, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  308 words  |  Billy Koelling

The Home Alone star, 42, and his fiancée actress Brenda Song, 34, gave their second baby a pretty solid name. The couple, who are known to keep their personal life private, has been secretive about the pregnancy and waited a few months before announcing the happy news. We congratulate this lovely duo, and we’d love to tell you more about their special approach to parenting which might come as a surprise given how famous they are.

They are now the proud parents of 2 boys.

Culkin and Song chose the name Carson for their second son, who was reportedly born around Christmas.

The couple became parents for the first time back in April 2021, after welcoming their oldest son, Dakota. The baby was named after Culkin’s sister, who died in a car accident in 2008. Back then, they also kept the news of their baby’s birth under wraps for a while and waited before announcing it to the world with a short statement saying that they were “overjoyed.”

They are fully devoted to their kids.

The 2 actors have been romantically linked since 2017, and they got engaged at the beginning of 2022, just a few months after Dakota’s birth.

And it seems that this pair is perfectly in sync when it comes to their parenting style. Song previously noted that they are “hands-on parents.”

Moreover, these young parents prefer to rely on family when they need some help with the baby. After their firstborn, Brenda explained, “We don’t have a nanny, but my mom has been here with us since my son was born.”

She added, “When I was working, my mom would bring [my son] to set so I could breastfeed and see him during the day.”

They are a solid couple.

The actress also insists on the importance of strong communication when it comes to raising a child together.

She shared, “I think when you and your partner have kids, especially the first few months, you don’t have the patience to be polite with each other.” She added, ’’Being communicative when you need help is so important. Instead of having set duties, we just feel each other out.’’
